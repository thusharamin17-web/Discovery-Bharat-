About UCM

University College Mangalore, formerly known as Government College, is one of the oldest and most distinguished institutions of higher learning in coastal Karnataka. Originally established as a Government institution, it has grown over decades into a premier constituent college of Mangalore University.

The college became a First Grade College, introducing Bachelor degrees in Arts and Commerce. In 1993, under the Karnataka Government, it was elevated to a constituent college of Mangalore University — marking a defining chapter in its history.

Today, UCM offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs across Arts, Science, Commerce, and Management. The institution is led by Prof. Ganapathi Gowda S as Principal, and is guided by the vision: "To be the centre for knowledge to all sections of the society."

Our Journey

History

1868
Government College Established

The college was established as a Government institution in Mangalore, laying the foundation of quality higher education in the coastal Karnataka region.

1902
Women Admitted

A landmark moment — women were admitted for the first time, progressively expanding the college's reach, diversity, and dynamism.

1948
First Grade College Status

The college was elevated to First Grade status with Bachelor degrees in Arts and Commerce introduced for students.

1993
University College Mangalore

The Government College became University College Mangalore — a constituent college of Mangalore University — marking a historic transformation.

2006–2009
New Programs Introduced

BBA (Travel & Tourism Management), Journalism, and other modern programs were introduced to keep pace with academic trends and industry demands.
